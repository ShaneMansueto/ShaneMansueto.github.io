DATA , INFORMATION and INFORMATION SYSTEMS

Portfolio-2

ANALYSIS

Data is essentially an input to a process that outputs meaningful information. Data by itself is hard to interpret without context and inspection. Since data can be raw and abstract processes would be necessary for understanding its relevance.

Data contains important details collected from or by the user and are then processed into information that the user is able to view and comprehend with much more ease. The more data available in a system the more accurate and comprehensible its output information becomes.

The processes of data collection, management and analysis are a substantial part in many systems (e.g., banking, management, and networking). Furthermore the process of accessing data is one of earliest step taken at the start of any computer program.

Information Systems manages everything needed to access and use data and datasets, from the collection of data, to the storing of data in datasets inside databases, to the processing of data, and its circulation.

Information Systems also includes everything needed to maintain its processes (e.g., hardware (CPUs, servers, etc...), system software, maintenance personnel, and data analysts).

There are six types of information systems TPS, OAS, KWS, MIS, DSS, and ESS. Each has their own different use cases, collected data and datasets, data processing method and information synthesized.

REACTION

Information systems are an integral part in modern society. We implore the use of computer computations to analyze complex datasets that would usually take weeks if not months to synthesize. Information systems make online transactions possible and usable. It takes a big network of servers in an information system to connect multiple people online.

In searching of references for information systems there is an apparent disparity of resources pertaining directly towards the courses IT and IS compared to computer science. Subjects relating to those courses fall under the CS umbrella but is difficult to know which subjects would directly fall under IT or IS.
