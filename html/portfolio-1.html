Computing Disciplines and Majors

Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Software Engineering, Information Technology, and Information Systems the 5 Computing disciplines. In which the disciplines Computer Science and Software Engineering deals with the development of computer software.

Where Computer Science leans more towards infrastructure software, which includes operating systems, tools, and platforms. On the other hand Software Engineering leans towards application software, which are the apps people are more familiar with.

In the hardware side of things exists Computer Engineering, which deals with developing physical computer chips and electronics, and integrates them with embedded-software. The last two disciplines, Information Technology and Information Systems deals with implementing and managing computer products and systems.

Computer Science

Subjects that falls under the Computer Science umbrella includes arguably the widest assortment of specified specialties among all disciplines, such as the inclusion of Cybersecurity, Machine Learning, Computing Algorithms, etc.. Ideal for persons with interest in computer research and development, especially theoretical aspects.

Computer Engineering

Computer Engineering includes learning both hardware and software, specifically embedded-software. Computer engineers are able to build, understand, and develop electronic parts and devices. They are responsible for building hardware for computers, servers, consoles, etc… The discipline includes subjects such as Embedded-programming, Electronics, Hardware Design, etc…

Software Engineering

Software Engineering, a discipline focused heavily in software design and application. A software may go through all processes from the start of the project to the implementation of their applications. They are responsible for creating apps that are easy to use for the general user. Having the need to learn Software Design, Software Analysis, etc…

Information Technology vs Information Systems

how do they differ?

Both similar in the aspect that they are farther away from the actual development of hardware and software than the other disciplines, and that they can fit the same roles, they still have different primary objectives. Whilst Information Technology focuses on maintaining, managing and implementing systems that process data, Information Systems work on the data and data processing itself, they do largely data analysis, interpretation and prediction.
